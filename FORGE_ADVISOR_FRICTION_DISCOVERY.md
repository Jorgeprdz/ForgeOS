# Forge Advisor Friction Discovery

Status: DISCOVERY

No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No UI. No Build Tree
changes. No architecture modifications. No scores. No labels.

## 1. Executive Summary

Advisor friction is the force that prevents the advisor from taking productive
action even when they know the action would help.

It follows similar human patterns as prospect friction:

- fear
- avoidance
- uncertainty
- complexity
- weak next step
- emotional discomfort
- lack of confidence
- competing motivation

## 2. Evidence

Kinder identifies resistance to telephone prospecting as commonly caused by
fear of rejection. The method recommends preparation, courage, scripts,
practice and action.

SMNYL uses induction, agenda, 25 points, role play, field observation and
partner feedback to reduce advisor-side friction.

Existing Forge Professional Development discoveries identify that growth
requires more than information. Behavior, accountability, repetition and
feedback matter.

## 3. Advisor Friction Types

### Prospecting Avoidance

Description:

Advisor knows calls or contacts are required but delays.

Likely hidden friction:

Fear of rejection, discomfort, weak confidence or lack of prepared language.

### Follow-Up Avoidance

Description:

Advisor delays reconnecting after a meeting.

Likely hidden friction:

Fear of seeming pushy, fear of hearing no, uncertainty about next step.

### Perfectionism

Description:

Advisor delays action until presentation, knowledge or material feels perfect.

Likely hidden friction:

Fear of being exposed as unprepared.

### Product Knowledge Anxiety

Description:

Advisor avoids product conversation because they fear not knowing enough.

Likely hidden friction:

Confidence gap, preparation gap or lack of field practice.

### Activity Inconsistency

Description:

Advisor has bursts of activity but lacks rhythm.

Likely hidden friction:

Weak habit system, competing priorities or avoidance disguised as busyness.

### Closing Discomfort

Description:

Advisor avoids asking for action even when prospect appears ready.

Likely hidden friction:

Fear of pressure, rejection or damaging rapport.

## 4. Advisor Friction vs Lack of Motivation

Advisor friction is often not lack of motivation.

It is often competing motivation:

- desire to succeed vs fear of rejection
- desire to help vs fear of pressure
- desire to be professional vs fear of imperfection
- desire for activity vs comfort of avoidance

## 5. Discovery Conclusion

Forge should understand advisor friction as development evidence, not as a
character flaw.

The purpose is not to shame the advisor into activity.

The purpose is to identify what prevents known productive action from happening.
