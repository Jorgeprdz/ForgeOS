# Forge Action Safety Signals Discovery

Status: DISCOVERY

No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No UI. No Build Tree
changes. No architecture modifications. No scores. No labels.

## 1. Executive Summary

Action Safety signals are observable evidence patterns suggesting that a person
feels safe enough to move from decision toward action.

They are not proof.

They are not compliance markers.

## 2. Prospect Action Safety Signals

Signals:

- willingness to choose among options
- willingness to commit money, time or attention
- willingness to schedule the next step
- willingness to disclose sensitive information
- willingness to involve spouse or decision-maker
- willingness to proceed despite bounded uncertainty
- asks implementation questions
- asks clarification rather than justification questions
- confirms the recommendation matches stated priorities
- identifies what would make them comfortable proceeding
- accepts tradeoffs without panic
- knows what happens after saying yes

Interpretation:

The prospect is not merely interested. They may feel action is safe enough to
explore execution.

## 3. Advisor Action Safety Signals

Signals:

- makes prospecting calls despite discomfort
- follows up without over-apologizing
- asks for action clearly
- uses prepared language without sounding robotic
- accepts possible rejection without identity collapse
- reviews activity honestly
- asks for coaching
- practices before live conversations
- acts before feeling fully confident

Interpretation:

The advisor feels safe enough to perform productive action despite fear of
rejection or imperfection.

## 4. Manager Action Safety Signals

Signals:

- holds difficult coaching conversation early
- names expectations clearly
- reviews behavior evidence without attacking personhood
- intervenes before the issue becomes severe
- distinguishes support from rescue
- lets the advisor own next steps
- follows up on commitments
- accepts short-term discomfort for long-term development

Interpretation:

The manager feels safe enough to lead through discomfort.

## 5. False Action Safety

False Action Safety appears when action happens, but not from responsible
safety.

Examples:

- blind confidence
- pressure-induced action
- social compliance
- emotional impulse
- desire to please advisor
- desire to escape the conversation
- overconfidence without understanding
- manager coercion
- advisor bravado

Difference:

Genuine Action Safety preserves agency, understanding and responsibility.

False Action Safety produces action without responsible ownership.

## 6. Signal Combinations

Stronger evidence appears when signals cluster:

- clarity + commitment + next step
- trust + disclosure + implementation questions
- fear acknowledged + action still chosen
- uncertainty named + uncertainty bounded
- decision-maker involved + economic clarity present

Weak evidence:

- enthusiasm alone
- "sounds good"
- social agreement
- quick yes under pressure
- action without understanding

## 7. Boundary

Forge should never treat Action Safety signals as permission to pressure a
person.

The ethical use of Action Safety is to understand what supports responsible
action, not to increase compliance.
