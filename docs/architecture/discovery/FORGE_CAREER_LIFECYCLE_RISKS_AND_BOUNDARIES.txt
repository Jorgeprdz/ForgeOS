FORGE CAREER LIFECYCLE RISKS AND BOUNDARIES

Status:
ARCHITECTURE DISCOVERY

Scope:
No implementation. No code. No UI. No schemas. No engines. No Build Tree
changes.

CORE RISK

Career Stage is powerful. If implemented carelessly, it can become a rigid,
company-specific, surveillance-heavy or compensation-driven label.

Forge must avoid turning a human career into a simplistic hierarchy.

BOUNDARY 1: CAREER STAGE IS NOT TITLE

Risk:
Local titles vary by company, channel, office and time.

Boundary:
Title may be evidence, but title is not stage truth.

BOUNDARY 2: CAREER STAGE IS NOT COMPENSATION

Risk:
Compensation structures may tempt Forge to infer stage from money or rule
eligibility.

Boundary:
Compensation can contribute evidence, but cannot own Career Stage.

BOUNDARY 3: CAREER STAGE IS NOT PRODUCTION ONLY

Risk:
Production-only models erase readiness, character, behavior, conservation,
learning, leadership and trust.

Boundary:
Production history is evidence, not the full definition of success.

BOUNDARY 4: LEADERSHIP STAGE IS NOT CAREER STAGE

Risk:
Manager, Subdirector and Director may be incorrectly treated as a linear
continuation of advisor maturity.

Boundary:
Leadership Stage is a separate dimension.

BOUNDARY 5: PARTNER IS NOT AUTOMATICALLY MANAGER

Risk:
Partner Intelligence could collapse into org chart logic.

Boundary:
Partner can mean leverage, trust, referrals, ecosystem and strategic
contribution, with or without team responsibility.

BOUNDARY 6: MANAGER INTELLIGENCE IS NOT SURVEILLANCE

Risk:
Career Stage may increase manager visibility into sensitive development
signals.

Boundary:
Manager Intelligence exists to develop people, not monitor or shame them.

BOUNDARY 7: PURPOSE IS NOT PRESSURE

Risk:
Purpose Snapshot may become a tool for pressure, guilt or sales coercion.

Boundary:
Purpose is human context, not a commercial pressure mechanism.

BOUNDARY 8: REGRESSION IS NOT ONE BAD MONTH

Risk:
Temporary performance drops may be mislabeled as career regression.

Boundary:
Distinguish risk, dormancy, reactivation and formal regression.

BOUNDARY 9: CANDIDATE IDENTITY IS DURABLE

Risk:
Failed precontract or reentry may overwrite a person incorrectly.

Boundary:
RecruitIdentity remains durable. Applications and cycles are attempts, not
permanent person identity.

BOUNDARY 10: CAREER STAGE IS NOT UNIVERSAL COMPANY HIERARCHY

Risk:
Forge Core may hardcode carrier, office, channel or company-specific stages.

Boundary:
Forge Core owns universal stage concepts. Organization profiles and rule packs
interpret local structures.

DANGEROUS ASSUMPTIONS

Forge must never assume:
- title equals stage
- manager equals partner
- partner equals manager
- production equals maturity
- compensation equals stage
- tenure equals maturity
- leadership title means leadership quality
- inactivity means permanent failure
- a failed cycle means failed person
- stage is static forever
- purpose is a measurable motivation score
- manager opinion overrides evidence

RISK SCENARIOS

Scenario 1:
Advisor has strong production for one month.

Bad inference:
Promote to Professional Advisor.

Correct handling:
Mark production signal and wait for durable evidence.

Scenario 2:
Partner has no team.

Bad inference:
Demote because no leadership responsibility.

Correct handling:
Partner may remain Career Stage while Leadership Stage stays Individual
Contributor.

Scenario 3:
Manager loses team.

Bad inference:
Demote career stage automatically.

Correct handling:
Leadership Stage may change; Career Stage requires separate review.

Scenario 4:
Candidate fails precontract.

Bad inference:
Erase person or mark permanently unfit.

Correct handling:
Preserve RecruitIdentity, close cycle, allow future reentry with evidence.

Scenario 5:
Purpose Snapshot shows family motivation.

Bad inference:
Use family driver to pressure production.

Correct handling:
Use as human context for supportive guidance only.

CONSTITUTIONAL BOUNDARIES TO CONSIDER

1. Career Stage requires evidence.
2. Leadership Stage is separate from Career Stage.
3. Compensation never owns Career Stage.
4. Production never fully defines maturity.
5. Manager visibility must serve development.
6. Purpose must never become pressure.
7. Local hierarchy belongs in organization profiles or rule packs, not Forge
   Core.
8. Regression requires review and evidence.
9. Dormancy and reactivation are operating states, not moral judgments.
10. Career OS must protect human dignity.

FINAL RISK VERDICT

Career Lifecycle can safely become a lock candidate only if Forge separates:

- Career Stage
- Operating State
- Leadership Stage
- Evidence
- Local organization interpretation

Verdict:
CANDIDATE FOR LOCK.
