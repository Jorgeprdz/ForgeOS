## FRICTION PATTERN 1

Name:

Affordability Uncertainty

Description:

The person recognizes the need but is unsure whether acting will damage cash
flow, lifestyle, liquidity or other obligations.

Evidence:

SMNYL objection examples include "no tengo dinero." Kinder requires monetary
commitment before recommendation and treats payment capacity as part of
prospect qualification.

Source:

SMNYL Methodology; Garry Kinder Methodology.

Confidence Level:

HIGH

## FRICTION PATTERN 2

Name:

Decision-Maker Absence

Description:

The person may be ready individually, but action stalls because spouse, partner,
family member or business decision-maker is not aligned or present.

Evidence:

Kinder emphasizes contacting who takes the decision and includes spouse
influence. Readiness discovery flagged missing decision-makers as a readiness
risk.

Source:

Garry Kinder Methodology; Existing Forge Readiness Discovery.

Confidence Level:

HIGH

## FRICTION PATTERN 3

Name:

Fear of Regret

Description:

The person delays because acting creates the possibility of later feeling they
made the wrong choice.

Evidence:

Kinder emphasizes simple, clear presentation because confused prospects rarely
buy. SMNYL includes requests like "mandamelo por correo," which can represent
information need or postponement.

Source:

Garry Kinder Methodology; SMNYL Methodology; Professional advisor experience.

Confidence Level:

MEDIUM-HIGH

## FRICTION PATTERN 4

Name:

Option Preservation

Description:

The person avoids commitment because committing feels like losing flexibility,
even when they believe action would help.

Evidence:

Readiness discovery distinguishes decision from action. Commitment can remain
tentative when the prospect has not accepted resource allocation.

Source:

Existing Forge Readiness Discovery; Professional advisor experience.

Confidence Level:

MEDIUM

## FRICTION PATTERN 5

Name:

Cognitive Overload

Description:

The person sees value but the decision feels too complex, technical or heavy to
complete now.

Evidence:

Kinder states that confused prospects rarely buy and that presentation power
depends on simplicity.

Source:

Garry Kinder Methodology.

Confidence Level:

HIGH

## FRICTION PATTERN 6

Name:

Emotional Avoidance

Description:

The recommendation forces the person to face mortality, illness, disability,
financial fragility or responsibility, so they delay despite agreement.

Evidence:

Pedro Camarena Intelligence centers catastrophic risks that can affect savings,
income and life goals. These risks are emotionally difficult to own.

Source:

Pedro Camarena Intelligence; Professional advisor experience.

Confidence Level:

MEDIUM-HIGH

## FRICTION PATTERN 7

Name:

Social Pressure Avoidance

Description:

The person fears being pushed, judged or manipulated, so they avoid action even
when the need is real.

Evidence:

Kinder contrasts professional selling with product-centered pressure. SMNYL
scripts often reassure prospects that the advisor is not calling to force a
purchase.

Source:

SMNYL Methodology; Garry Kinder Methodology.

Confidence Level:

HIGH

## FRICTION PATTERN 8

Name:

Administrative Drag

Description:

The person decides but stalls at documents, forms, payment setup, signatures,
medical requirements or scheduling.

Evidence:

Decision vs transaction discovery separates decision, application,
implementation and policy placement. Action can fail after decision.

Source:

Existing Forge Decision vs Transaction Discovery; Professional advisor
experience.

Confidence Level:

MEDIUM-HIGH

## FRICTION PATTERN 9

Name:

Weak Next Step

Description:

The person agrees in principle but no specific, owned next action exists.

Evidence:

SMNYL and Kinder both emphasize scheduling the next appointment and moving from
need to monetary commitment and closing appointment.

Source:

SMNYL Methodology; Garry Kinder Methodology.

Confidence Level:

HIGH

## FRICTION PATTERN 10

Name:

Advisor Follow-Up Avoidance

Description:

The client may be ready, but the advisor delays the uncomfortable follow-up or
fails to lead the next step.

Evidence:

Kinder identifies advisor resistance to phone calls as often caused by fear of
rejection. SMNYL uses activity systems and 25 points to keep action visible.

Source:

Garry Kinder Methodology; SMNYL Methodology.

Confidence Level:

HIGH

## FRICTION PATTERN 12

Name:

Information-as-Delay

Description:

The person requests more information not to understand, but to postpone action.

Evidence:

SMNYL includes "mandamelo por correo" as an objection pattern. Kinder separates
understanding from delay and emphasizes clarity before close.

Source:

SMNYL Methodology; Garry Kinder Methodology.

Confidence Level:

MEDIUM-HIGH
