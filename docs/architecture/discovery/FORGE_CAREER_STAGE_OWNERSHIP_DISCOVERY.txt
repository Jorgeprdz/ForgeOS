FORGE CAREER STAGE OWNERSHIP DISCOVERY

Status:
ARCHITECTURE DISCOVERY

Scope:
No implementation. No schemas. No engines. No UI. No code. No Build Tree
changes.

CORE QUESTION

Who owns Career Stage in Forge?

DISCOVERY ANSWER

Career Stage should become a first-class architectural primitive, but no current
business domain should silently own it alone.

Recommended ownership pattern:

Career Lifecycle Context should own canonical stage state.

Other domains should contribute evidence and consume the official stage output.

CONCEPTUAL OWNERSHIP MODEL

Career Lifecycle Context:
- owns canonical careerStage
- owns operatingState
- owns stage transition history
- owns effective period
- owns stage confidence
- owns stage evidence references

Leadership Context:
- owns leadershipStage
- owns leadership assignment state
- owns manager/subdirector/director responsibility context
- owns effective period
- owns evidence references

Recruitment Intelligence:
- contributes candidate evidence
- contributes assessment evidence
- contributes interview evidence
- does not own long-term Career Stage

Precontract Intelligence:
- contributes precontract cycle evidence
- contributes activation/readiness evidence
- contributes conversion evidence
- does not overwrite durable identity

Advisor Experience:
- consumes Career Stage
- adapts Benvenù, Future You, Alfred and Progressive Discovery
- does not own Career Stage

Mick / Behavior Intelligence:
- contributes behavior evidence
- interprets behavior by stage
- does not promote or demote stage by itself

Productivity Intelligence:
- contributes productivity evidence
- does not equate production with maturity

Business Planning:
- consumes Career Stage
- adapts plans to stage
- contributes planning maturity evidence
- does not own stage

Forecast Intelligence:
- consumes Career Stage
- interprets forecast by stage
- does not create stage

Compensation Intelligence:
- contributes compensation/rule evidence when applicable
- consumes Career Stage for interpretation
- does not own stage

Manager Intelligence:
- consumes Career Stage and Leadership Stage
- contributes coaching/development evidence
- does not use manager opinion as stage truth without evidence

Partner Intelligence:
- contributes partner leverage and network maturity evidence
- does not equate partner stage with org title

Rule Packs / Organization Profiles:
- map local titles, channels, rules and hierarchy terms
- do not become Forge Core
- may interpret evidence in context

Human Review:
- required when stage evidence conflicts
- required when local title and observed maturity disagree
- required when promotion/regression affects sensitive outputs

WHY NO SINGLE DOMAIN SHOULD OWN IT

Recruitment sees entry, not the whole career.

Precontract sees readiness, not mature practice.

Productivity sees output, not identity.

Compensation sees rules and money, not human maturity.

Manager Intelligence sees people-development context, but may be biased by
organizational needs.

Partner Intelligence sees leverage, not all leadership responsibility.

Career Stage cuts across all of them.

CAREER STAGE VS OPERATING STATE

Career Stage:
Durable lifecycle context.

Examples:
- Candidate
- Precontract
- Advisor
- Professional Advisor
- Partner
- Senior Partner
- Managing Partner

Operating State:
Current condition inside a stage.

Examples:
- active
- inactive
- dormant
- at risk
- reactivation
- in review
- transitioning

CAREER STAGE VS LEADERSHIP STAGE

Career Stage:
Professional maturity and career lifecycle.

Leadership Stage:
Organizational responsibility for people, teams or units.

Examples:
- Individual Contributor
- Manager
- Subdirector
- Director

These must be separate.

A person may be Partner + Individual Contributor.

A person may be Advisor + Manager.

A person may be Professional Advisor + Subdirector.

A person may be Managing Partner + Director.

EVIDENCE OWNERSHIP

Stage evidence should include:
- source
- date
- domain
- confidence
- effective period
- human review status when ambiguous

Evidence examples:
- application status
- precontract cycle status
- contract signed
- advisor conversion
- production history
- activity history
- tenure
- relationship/practice evidence
- leadership assignment
- team responsibility
- development activity
- recruiting activity
- organization profile
- rule pack interpretation

NON-EVIDENCE

Do not treat these as stage truth alone:
- job title
- one high production month
- one low production month
- compensation potential
- forecast
- self-description
- manager preference
- CRM pipeline stage
- profile field
- UI selection

RECOMMENDED CONCEPTUAL CONTRACT

No schema approved, but the conceptual contract should include:

- personId
- careerStage
- operatingState
- leadershipStage
- effectiveFrom
- effectiveTo
- evidence[]
- confidence
- sourceDomain
- reviewStatus
- rulePackContext when applicable

FINAL OWNERSHIP VERDICT

Career Stage should be owned by a dedicated Career Lifecycle Context or Career
Intelligence foundation, not by CRM, Compensation, Manager Intelligence or
Advisor Experience.

Verdict:
CANDIDATE FOR LOCK.
