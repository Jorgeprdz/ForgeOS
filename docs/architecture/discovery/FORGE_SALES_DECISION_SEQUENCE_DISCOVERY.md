# Forge Sales Decision Sequence Discovery

Status: DISCOVERY

No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No UI. No Build Tree
changes. No architecture modifications.

## 1. Executive Summary

The sale does not occur at one point.

Across the three sources, the sale is a sequence of decision transitions:

1. appointment decision
2. trust decision
3. relevance decision
4. need recognition decision
5. disclosure decision
6. monetary commitment decision
7. recommendation decision
8. application / action decision
9. relationship continuation decision
10. referral decision

## 2. Advisor Process vs Prospect Decision

Advisor process:

- prospect
- contact
- schedule
- discover
- recommend
- close
- deliver
- ask for referrals

Prospect decision:

- listen
- trust
- care
- disclose
- prioritize
- commit
- evaluate
- act
- remain
- recommend

Forge should preserve this distinction in future architecture discovery.

## 3. Source Comparison

### Pedro Camarena Intelligence

Sequence:

Credibility -> recommendation-based trust -> risk theory -> catastrophic risk
recognition -> risk transfer logic -> product category mapping -> prospect
choice of risk to administer.

Sale location:

The sale begins when the prospect accepts that the risk exists and should be
administered.

### Corporate Methodology

Sequence:

Activity -> qualified names -> appointment -> approach -> ANF -> most important
need -> monetary commitment -> closing appointment -> referral generation ->
field development.

Sale location:

The sale is operationalized through appointment, needs analysis, commitment and
follow-up discipline.

### Kinder Methodology

Sequence:

Six sales:

1. appointment under favorable conditions
2. sell advisor, company, need recognition and service
3. monetary commitment
4. presentation and close
5. solidify sale and relationship
6. make sale productive through referrals

Sale location:

Every stage contains a sale because every stage requires a buyer decision.

## 4. Decision Transition Model

### Transition 1: From Name to Contact

Question:

"Is this person reachable with enough context to approach professionally?"

Evidence:

- SMNYL Project 200 and NAST.
- Kinder contact / referred qualified distinction.

### Transition 2: From Contact to Appointment

Question:

"Will the person give attention under favorable conditions?"

Evidence:

- SMNYL phone and WhatsApp approach.
- Kinder appointment under favorable conditions.

### Transition 3: From Appointment to Trust

Question:

"Will the prospect allow the advisor into a meaningful conversation?"

Evidence:

- Pedro personal introduction and credentials.
- Kinder selling self and company.
- SMNYL agenda and professionalism.

### Transition 4: From Trust to Need Recognition

Question:

"Does the prospect recognize a need, risk or desired future?"

Evidence:

- Pedro risk categories and transfer.
- SMNYL ANF.
- Kinder strategic questions.

### Transition 5: From Need Recognition to Commitment

Question:

"Does the prospect care enough and have capacity enough to continue?"

Evidence:

- SMNYL most important need and monetary commitment.
- Kinder prospect qualified definition and monetary commitment.

### Transition 6: From Commitment to Recommendation

Question:

"Can the advisor prepare a recommendation based on the prospect's facts,
feelings and capacity?"

Evidence:

- Kinder between-citas recommendation preparation.
- SMNYL closing appointment after ANF.

### Transition 7: From Recommendation to Action

Question:

"Does the prospect believe this recommendation fits and should be acted on now?"

Evidence:

- Kinder presentation, close and buyer assistance.
- SMNYL close interviews and product work.

### Transition 8: From Action to Relationship

Question:

"Does the prospect become a continuing client rather than a one-time buyer?"

Evidence:

- Kinder solidify sale and develop relationship.
- SMNYL referral generation and ongoing advisor discipline.

### Transition 9: From Relationship to Referral

Question:

"Did the experience create enough value and trust that the client recommends
others?"

Evidence:

- Pedro works by recommendation.
- SMNYL referral scripts.
- Kinder productive sale through referrals.

## 5. When Does the Sale Really Occur?

If "sale" means transaction:

It occurs at close / application / policy placement.

If "sale" means human decision:

It occurs progressively.

The most important pre-transaction sale is:

Need recognition plus trust plus monetary commitment.

Without that, the final presentation is often only a pitch.

## 6. Durable Sequence

Durable professional sequence:

Qualified attention -> trust -> need recognition -> personal ownership ->
capacity -> commitment -> recommendation fit -> action -> relationship ->
referral.

This sequence remains valid beyond insurance.
