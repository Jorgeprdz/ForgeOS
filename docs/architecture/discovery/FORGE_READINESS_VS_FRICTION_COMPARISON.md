# Forge Readiness vs Friction Comparison

Status: DISCOVERY

No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No UI. No Build Tree
changes. No architecture modifications. No scores. No labels.

## 1. Executive Summary

Readiness and friction are not opposites.

They can coexist.

Readiness asks:

"Is the person prepared to move toward action?"

Friction asks:

"What force is preventing or slowing that action?"

A person can be ready and blocked at the same time.

## 2. Readiness Signals

Readiness signals include:

- need ownership
- open disclosure
- monetary commitment
- decision-maker involvement
- implementation questions
- option comparison
- specific next step
- clarification instead of justification

These suggest the person may be moving toward action.

## 3. Friction Signals

Friction signals include:

- procrastination
- delayed documents
- vague follow-up
- repeated rescheduling
- endless research
- spouse dependency
- fear of commitment
- confusion
- overwhelm
- missing payment clarity
- "send it to me" without next step
- agreement without completion

These suggest action is being slowed, blocked or avoided.

## 4. Can Both Exist Simultaneously?

Yes.

Examples:

High readiness + financial friction:

The prospect owns the need but is unsure how much can be allocated.

High readiness + relational friction:

The prospect wants to act but spouse or business partner must be included.

High readiness + cognitive friction:

The prospect accepts the need but does not understand the recommendation well
enough to proceed.

High readiness + emotional friction:

The prospect knows action is needed but feels fear, grief, shame or regret.

High readiness + administrative friction:

The prospect says yes but delays documents, forms or payment.

## 5. High Readiness Can Coexist With...

Fear:

Yes. A person can decide action is needed and still fear consequences,
commitment or regret.

Uncertainty:

Yes. Readiness does not mean total certainty. It means enough ownership to move,
if uncertainty is manageable.

Lack of clarity:

Yes, but only temporarily. If clarity remains unresolved, readiness may decay.

Lack of money:

Yes, but then action must adapt to the constraint. If no economic capacity
exists, transaction may be impossible despite readiness.

Missing decision-makers:

Yes. Individual readiness may exist without household or business readiness.

## 6. Readiness vs False Readiness vs Friction

Readiness:

"I see it, own it and am prepared to move."

False readiness:

"I seem agreeable, but I have not owned the decision."

Friction:

"I own the decision, but something is making action hard."

## 7. Relationship to Action

Readiness without friction:

Action can proceed.

Readiness with friction:

Action requires friction resolution.

Friction without readiness:

The person is not blocked from acting; they are not ready to act.

False readiness with friction:

Advisor may misdiagnose the situation and push incorrectly.

## 8. Discovery Conclusion

Friction Intelligence should not replace Readiness Intelligence.

It should explain why readiness fails to become action.
