READINESS SIGNALS AND ANTI-SIGNALS

Status:
ARCHITECTURE DISCOVERY

Scope:
No implementation. No code. No UI. No schemas. No engines. No scores. No
rankings. No Build Tree changes.

CORE PRINCIPLE

Readiness is evidence-supported confidence for a specific responsibility.

SIGNALS

1. Responsibility-specific capability

Meaning:
The person has demonstrated the actual capability required by the next
responsibility.

Example:
Before managing others, they have shown coaching, judgment and follow-through
with development responsibility.

2. Consistent follow-through

Meaning:
The person repeatedly closes loops and honors commitments.

Why it matters:
Readiness requires durability, not one strong moment.

3. Judgment under context

Meaning:
The person makes appropriate decisions when conditions are real, ambiguous or
imperfect.

Evidence:
- asks for help when needed
- avoids unsupported claims
- understands risk
- chooses proportionate next actions

4. Accountability

Meaning:
The person owns commitments, outcomes and recovery behavior.

Evidence:
- admits missed commitments
- recovers
- follows up transparently
- does not hide uncertainty

5. Trust evidence

Meaning:
The person can be trusted with clients, candidates, advisors, teams or strategic
relationships.

Evidence:
- reliability
- honesty
- stewardship
- boundary respect
- no manipulation

6. Coachability in action

Meaning:
Feedback changes behavior.

Anti-pattern:
Agreement without change.

7. Development trend

Meaning:
The person is improving in the capability needed for the next responsibility.

Evidence:
- repeated improvement
- fewer repeated mistakes
- stronger decision quality
- more stable cadence

8. Recovery after setback

Meaning:
The person can respond to difficulty without collapse, avoidance or blame.

Why it matters:
Greater responsibility creates stress. Recovery behavior matters.

9. Evidence of stewardship

Meaning:
The person protects the people or assets entrusted to them.

Examples:
- client trust
- candidate dignity
- advisor development
- partner network quality

10. Human review when ambiguous

Meaning:
When signals conflict, reviewed judgment is needed.

Why it matters:
Readiness can affect real responsibility and trust.

ANTI-SIGNALS

1. Tenure alone

Tenure means time passed. It does not prove readiness.

2. Production alone

Production can show output. It does not prove maturity, trust or judgment.

3. Confidence alone

Confidence may be ahead of competence.

4. Activity volume alone

Activity can be noisy, low-quality or unsustained.

5. Information or training completion alone

Knowing is not handling responsibility.

6. Certification alone

Certification may show exposure or formal requirement, not real readiness.

7. Charisma

Charisma can mask weak follow-through or poor judgment.

8. Popularity

Popularity is not trustworthiness.

9. Manager preference

Manager preference can be biased or politically shaped.

10. Compensation level

Compensation is an economic outcome under rules, not proof of readiness.

11. Title

Title may reflect assignment, not capability.

12. Team size

Team size may reflect responsibility, not leadership readiness.

13. One strong month

Momentum is not durable readiness.

14. Purpose Snapshot

Purpose is human context, not readiness evidence by itself.

FALSE READINESS PATTERNS

Pattern 1:
High production + weak trust.

Risk:
Promoting output while ignoring client or team risk.

Pattern 2:
High confidence + low competence.

Risk:
Responsibility without actual capability.

Pattern 3:
Long tenure + low growth.

Risk:
Assuming time created maturity.

Pattern 4:
Manager preference + weak evidence.

Risk:
Bias-driven promotion.

Pattern 5:
Training completed + no behavior change.

Risk:
Information mistaken for readiness.

Pattern 6:
Activity burst + no consistency.

Risk:
Temporary momentum mistaken for durability.

RECOMMENDED SIGNAL STRUCTURE

For any readiness judgment, require:

- responsibility
- context
- evidence
- confidence
- risks
- missing evidence
- recommended next step

FINAL SIGNAL VERDICT

CANDIDATE FOR LOCK.

Reason:
Readiness signals are coherent only when tied to responsibility-specific
evidence and protected against anti-signals.
