## 1. Purpose

Clarify the difference between Potential, Readiness and Excellence inside Forge
Career OS.

These concepts are related, but not equivalent.

Confusing them creates premature promotion, under-trust, poor coaching,
surveillance risk and bad career decisions.

## 2. Potential

Definition:

Potential is what a person could become.

Potential is future-oriented.

Potential asks:

"What might this person be capable of if developed well?"

Evidence may include:

- character signals
- responsiveness
- learning posture
- market quality
- early discipline
- reflection
- coachability signals
- adversity response
- early relationship quality

Potential does not prove:

- current capability
- readiness for responsibility
- excellence
- future success

Risk:

Potential can become fantasy if not grounded in evidence.

## 3. Readiness

Definition:

Readiness is what a person can responsibly handle today.

Readiness is present-context-oriented.

Readiness asks:

"Can this person be trusted with this specific responsibility now?"

Evidence may include:

- responsibility-specific capability
- follow-through
- judgment
- accountability
- trust
- consistency
- coachability in action
- recovery after setback
- development trend
- human review where ambiguous

Readiness does not prove:

- long-term excellence
- broad capability across all responsibilities
- permanent maturity
- universal promotion eligibility

Risk:

Readiness can become too broad if Forge does not ask "ready for what?"

## 4. Excellence

Definition:

Excellence is how well a person performs within a responsibility.

Excellence is quality-oriented.

Excellence asks:

"How well is this person operating inside this role or responsibility?"

Evidence may include:

- discipline
- consistency
- follow-through
- relationship quality
- trust
- decision quality
- adaptability
- business planning
- execution quality
- people stewardship

Excellence does not prove:

- readiness for the next responsibility
- desire for advancement
- leadership capability
- stage transition by itself

Risk:

Excellence in one responsibility may be mistaken for readiness for a different
responsibility.

## 5. Comparison

```text
Potential  = what could become true
Readiness  = what can be responsibly handled now
Excellence = how well current responsibility is performed
```

## 6. Examples

High Potential / Low Readiness:

A candidate has strong learning posture and character, but insufficient
follow-through evidence for advisor responsibility.

High Readiness / Developing Excellence:

An advisor is ready to manage a new responsibility but is not yet excellent in
that responsibility.

High Excellence / Low Next-Stage Readiness:

A professional advisor is excellent with clients but not ready to manage other
advisors.

High Results / Low Readiness:

An advisor has a strong production month but weak trust or product boundary
evidence.

High Confidence / Low Readiness:

A person believes they can lead, but has not shown accountability or judgment
with others.

## 7. Relationship to Career Stage

Career Stage says:

"Where is this person in the lifecycle?"

Potential says:

"What might this person become?"

Readiness says:

"What can this person responsibly handle now?"

Excellence says:

"How well is this person performing within responsibility?"

Professional Development says:

"How is this person growing?"

## 8. Constitutional Boundary

Forge must never use Potential, Readiness or Excellence interchangeably.

Potential without readiness should lead to development, not responsibility.

Readiness without excellence should lead to supported assignment, not assumed
mastery.

Excellence without next-stage readiness should lead to recognition and targeted
development, not automatic promotion.

## 9. Final Verdict

CANDIDATE FOR LOCK.

Reason:

The distinction is essential for Career OS to responsibly recommend trust,
delegation, promotion, coaching and development.
