# Forge Action Safety Model Candidate

Status: DISCOVERY

No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No UI. No Build Tree
changes. No architecture modifications. No scores. No labels.

## 1. Executive Summary

This is a discovery model candidate only.

It does not create architecture, scoring, labels or implementation.

## 2. Candidate Definition

Action Safety:

The evidence-supported condition in which a person feels sufficiently safe to
move from decision to responsible action despite remaining uncertainty.

## 3. Candidate Formula

Responsible Action becomes more likely when:

Readiness + Sufficient Action Safety + Available Path

are all present.

This is not a computational formula. It is a conceptual relationship.

## 4. Candidate Components

Action Safety may require enough of:

- trust
- clarity
- emotional safety
- economic clarity
- social safety
- relational alignment
- decision-maker alignment
- competence confidence
- implementation confidence
- agency preservation
- acceptable uncertainty

## 5. Candidate Destroyers

Action Safety is weakened by:

- confusion
- overwhelm
- fear of regret
- fear of embarrassment
- fear of rejection
- fear of pressure
- fear of losing options
- fear of conflict
- missing decision-maker
- economic ambiguity
- weak next step
- low implementation confidence

## 6. Candidate Signals

Evidence that Action Safety may exist:

- willingness to choose
- willingness to commit
- willingness to schedule
- willingness to disclose
- willingness to proceed despite uncertainty
- willingness to involve decision-makers
- implementation questions
- clarification questions
- concrete next step
- acknowledged fear plus chosen action

## 7. False Action Safety

False Action Safety includes:

- blind confidence
- pressure-induced action
- social compliance
- emotional impulse
- action to please someone
- action to escape discomfort
- action without understanding

False safety produces movement without responsible ownership.

## 8. Certainty Question

Does a person need certainty to act?

No.

People can act without certainty when uncertainty is:

- named
- bounded
- acceptable
- proportional
- supported by trust
- connected to a clear next step

Uncertainty becomes unsafe when:

- consequences are unclear
- costs are unclear
- decision-makers are missing
- implementation is opaque
- the person feels pressured
- downside is unbounded

## 9. Decision Quality Relationship

Can a good decision fail without Action Safety?

Yes.

A recommendation can be sound, appropriate and desired, but action can fail
because the person lacks economic clarity, relational alignment, implementation
confidence or emotional safety.

Can Action Safety exist around a poor decision?

Yes.

People can feel safe acting on a poor decision when they are overconfident,
pressured, socially compliant, emotionally impulsive or insufficiently informed.

Therefore, Action Safety is necessary but not sufficient for decision quality.

## 10. Universality Candidate

Action Safety appears to apply across:

- clients
- advisors
- managers
- partners
- recruits

But the components differ by context.

Client:

Will this decision harm my money, family, agency or future options?

Advisor:

Will this action expose me to rejection, embarrassment or failure?

Manager:

Will this conversation create conflict, disengagement or loss of control?

Recruit:

Will this career step expose me to uncertainty, identity risk or financial
instability?

Partner:

Will this responsibility create risk I cannot support or control?

## 11. Support and Refutation of Major Statement

Statement:

"People do not need perfect certainty. They need sufficient safety to act."

Support:

- Readiness Discovery showed uncertainty can coexist with readiness.
- Friction Discovery showed action stalls when danger feels unresolved.
- Kinder emphasizes clarity, trust and simplicity rather than certainty.
- SMNYL reduces fear of pressure and clarifies process before action.

Refutation:

- Some decisions require more than safety; they require evidence, suitability,
  authority and capacity.
- Safety can be false under pressure or impulse.
- Some people act without safety due to coercion, desperation or compliance.
- Some people need actual certainty in regulated or high-stakes contexts before
  action is responsible.

Balanced finding:

People do not need perfect certainty for many responsible actions, but they do
need enough safety, evidence and agency for action to be ethically sound.

## 12. Final Candidate

Action Safety should remain a discovery candidate.

It is a strong explanatory concept for why people fail to act despite knowing
and wanting to act.

It should not be used to increase compliance.

It should be used to understand what allows responsible human action.
