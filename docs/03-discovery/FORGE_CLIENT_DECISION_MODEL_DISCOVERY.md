# Forge Client Decision Model Discovery

Status: DISCOVERY

No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No UI. No Build Tree
changes. No architecture modifications.

## 1. Executive Summary

The prospect does not experience the sales process as a sequence of advisor
tasks.

The prospect experiences it as a sequence of internal questions:

1. Is this person worth listening to?
2. Is this conversation safe?
3. Does this matter to me?
4. Am I exposed to something real?
5. Do I understand the consequence?
6. Do I trust this person enough to disclose information?
7. Can I afford to act?
8. Do I believe the recommendation fits me?
9. Am I ready to commit?
10. What happens after I say yes?

## 2. Prospect Perspective

The prospect is not initially buying a product.

The prospect is trying to protect:

- identity
- family
- lifestyle
- liquidity
- future options
- peace of mind
- self-image as responsible person
- trust in their own judgment

## 3. Decision Sequence From the Prospect's View

### Stage 1: Permission to Listen

Internal question:

"Why should I give this person time?"

Sources:

- Pedro: recommendation-based credibility and personal introduction.
- SMNYL: approach scripts and reason for appointment.
- Kinder: appointment under favorable conditions.

Required belief:

The conversation might be worth the time.

### Stage 2: Safety and Credibility

Internal question:

"Can I trust this person enough to continue?"

Sources:

- Pedro: license, personal story, field purpose.
- SMNYL: company credibility, professional agenda.
- Kinder: sell yourself and company before deeper discussion.

Required belief:

The advisor is credible, professional and not merely trying to push a product.

### Stage 3: Personal Relevance

Internal question:

"Does this problem apply to me?"

Sources:

- Pedro: everyone faces risk by being alive.
- SMNYL: needs analysis and financial goals.
- Kinder: helping the prospect recognize need.

Required belief:

There is a real issue in my life worth examining.

### Stage 4: Consequence Recognition

Internal question:

"What happens if I do nothing?"

Sources:

- Pedro: catastrophic risks can damage life savings and economic stability.
- SMNYL: ANF explores needs, goals and current situation.
- Kinder: strategic questions uncover facts and feelings.

Required belief:

Inaction has a cost.

### Stage 5: Emotional Ownership

Internal question:

"Do I care enough to act?"

Sources:

- Pedro: asks which risk the prospect wants administered.
- SMNYL: asks for most important need.
- Kinder: need must become desire before money discussion.

Required belief:

This is not abstract. It is mine.

### Stage 6: Economic Possibility

Internal question:

"Can I realistically do something about it?"

Sources:

- SMNYL: solvency and monetary commitment.
- Kinder: capacity of payment and monetary commitment.
- Pedro: risk transfer must map to practical financial tools.

Required belief:

Action is financially possible.

### Stage 7: Advisor Fit

Internal question:

"Do I want this person to help me?"

Sources:

- Pedro: recommendation-based trust and field value.
- SMNYL: professionalism, agenda, interaction and follow-through.
- Kinder: liking, trust and advisor relationship as purchase drivers.

Required belief:

This advisor is the right guide.

### Stage 8: Recommendation Fit

Internal question:

"Does this recommendation make sense for me?"

Sources:

- SMNYL: needs analysis before close.
- Kinder: recommendation prepared after facts, feelings and commitment.
- Pedro: product follows risk category.

Required belief:

The recommendation is connected to my stated situation.

### Stage 9: Commitment

Internal question:

"Am I ready to act now?"

Sources:

- SMNYL: close appointment and commitments.
- Kinder: close as one stage in the six-sale process.
- Pedro: choose which risk to administer.

Required belief:

Action is better than delay.

### Stage 10: Post-Decision Confidence

Internal question:

"Did I make the right decision, and will this person remain with me?"

Sources:

- Kinder: solidify sale and develop relationship.
- SMNYL: referrals, follow-through and advisor activity.
- Pedro: recommendation-based practice depends on value delivered.

Required belief:

The advisor relationship continues after the transaction.

## 4. What the Prospect Thinks

Likely thoughts:

- I am busy.
- I do not want to be sold.
- I may already have something.
- I do not know if this applies to me.
- I do not want to expose personal finances without trust.
- I need to know if this is affordable.
- I need to know whether this advisor understands me.

## 5. What the Prospect Feels

Likely feelings:

- skepticism
- time pressure
- discomfort around risk
- fear of being pressured
- desire to be responsible
- concern for family
- relief when the problem becomes clear
- caution around money
- trust when the advisor listens

## 6. What the Prospect Fears

Likely fears:

- being manipulated
- wasting time
- being judged
- admitting financial gaps
- discovering an uncomfortable risk
- paying for something unnecessary
- making a wrong decision
- being abandoned after purchase

## 7. What the Prospect Must Believe Before Acting

Before action, the prospect must believe:

- the advisor is credible
- the conversation is about them, not only the product
- the risk or need is real
- doing nothing has a consequence
- the recommendation fits their situation
- the financial commitment is possible
- the advisor will remain accountable
- action now is better than deferral

## 8. Final Model

The client decision model is:

Attention -> Trust -> Relevance -> Consequence -> Ownership -> Capacity ->
Advisor Fit -> Recommendation Fit -> Commitment -> Post-Decision Confidence
