# Forge False Readiness Discovery

Status: DISCOVERY

No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No UI. No Build Tree
changes. No architecture modifications. No readiness scores. No readiness
labels.

## 1. Executive Summary

False readiness occurs when an advisor interprets weak or ambiguous signals as a
buying decision.

The danger is high because many prospects are polite, curious, agreeable or
avoid conflict. Those behaviors can look like readiness without producing
action.

## 2. False Readiness Signals

### Interest Without Commitment

Pattern:

The prospect asks questions but does not commit time, money, documents, next
meeting or decision-maker involvement.

Why it is false:

Curiosity is not decision.

### Agreement Without Ownership

Pattern:

The prospect says the topic is important but cannot describe why it matters
personally.

Why it is false:

Generic agreement is not personal need recognition.

### Politeness Mistaken for Intent

Pattern:

The prospect is friendly, attentive and complimentary but avoids concrete next
steps.

Why it is false:

Positive rapport can exist without buying readiness.

### Curiosity Without Urgency

Pattern:

The prospect wants information but does not see consequence in delay.

Why it is false:

Information-seeking is not action readiness.

### Advisor Projection

Pattern:

The advisor believes the prospect is ready because the case seems obvious to the
advisor.

Why it is false:

Advisor conviction does not equal prospect ownership.

### Budget Ambiguity

Pattern:

The prospect avoids monetary commitment or says "let me see options first" in a
way that hides capacity.

Why it is false:

Without economic reality, recommendation may become hypothetical.

### Missing Decision-Maker

Pattern:

One person appears ready, but spouse, partner or business owner is absent.

Why it is false:

Individual readiness may not equal household or business decision readiness.

### Referral Compliance

Pattern:

The prospect gives names to be helpful but has not internalized the value of the
process.

Why it is false:

Referral behavior can be social compliance, not conviction.

## 3. Not Yet Decided Signals

A prospect has likely not decided when they are still debating:

- whether the risk exists
- whether the risk applies to them
- whether action is necessary
- whether the advisor is credible
- whether the company is trustworthy
- whether they can afford anything
- whether the decision-maker should be involved
- whether the problem is urgent

These differ from objections.

Objections can occur after readiness.

Not-yet-decided signals occur before readiness.

## 4. Objection vs Not-Decided

Objection:

"I understand this matters, but I have a concern about cost, timing or fit."

Not decided:

"I am not sure this matters to me."

Objection:

"Can we adjust the amount?"

Not decided:

"Why would I need this at all?"

Objection:

"I need my spouse involved."

Not decided:

"I do not know if this conversation is worth continuing."

## 5. How False Readiness Differs From True Readiness

True readiness usually includes:

- ownership
- consequence
- disclosure
- commitment
- specific next step
- action-oriented questions

False readiness usually includes:

- agreement
- interest
- politeness
- vague next steps
- no money discussion
- no decision-maker clarity
- no personal consequence

## 6. Boundary

Forge should never convert false readiness signals into pressure.

False readiness discovery exists to protect the client and the advisor from
mistaking pleasant conversation for a responsible buying decision.
