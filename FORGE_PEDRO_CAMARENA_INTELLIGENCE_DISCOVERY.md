## 1. Executive Summary

Pedro Camarena Intelligence, represented by Guion Riesgos, is a field-tested
risk-recognition narrative.

It does not begin by explaining a product.

It begins by making risk visible.

The central move is:

People already live with risks. Some risks cannot be eliminated. Some cannot be
fully reduced. The healthiest response is to transfer the economic consequence
to an institution designed to carry it.

## 2. Narrative Structure

The source follows this sequence:

1. Thank the prospect for the opportunity.
2. Present advisor and practice.
3. Establish experience and professional legitimacy.
4. Explain recommendation-based work.
5. Introduce risk theory.
6. Classify risks as mild, moderate and catastrophic.
7. Define catastrophic economically.
8. Explain eliminate, reduce and transfer.
9. Identify everyday human risks.
10. Map those risks to transfer mechanisms.
11. Ask which risk the person wants administered.

## 3. Durable Intelligence

Durable principles:

- A person does not need to want a product to need risk administration.
- Risk exists before awareness.
- Catastrophic risk is economic before it is product-specific.
- The advisor creates value by helping the prospect recognize exposure.
- Risk transfer is a human decision before it is an insurance decision.
- The final question should invite prioritization, not force product purchase.

## 4. Underlying Human Problem

The human problem is:

People are exposed to risks that can damage savings, income, family stability
and goals, but they may not recognize those risks early enough to act.

## 5. What Occurs Before Product Discussion?

Before product discussion:

- the advisor establishes identity and credibility
- the advisor frames service as risk and savings administration
- the prospect is introduced to risk categories
- the prospect is shown why some risk cannot be controlled
- the concept of transfer is introduced

Products appear only after risk categories are understood.

## 6. Prospect Qualification View

Pedro does not use a formal qualification acronym in the extracted source.

However, the implied qualified prospect is someone who:

- has exposure to one or more risks
- can recognize the relevance of those risks
- is willing to discuss which risk should be administered
- may value advice enough to accept a recommendation-based conversation

## 7. Strengths

Strengths:

- starts from human reality, not product
- creates immediate relevance
- frames insurance as risk transfer
- reduces product clutter
- makes the advisor a guide through already-existing exposure
- creates a clean bridge to GMM, disability, life, retirement and goals

## 8. Risks or Limitations

Risks:

- may underemphasize formal qualification and economic capacity if used alone
- may rely heavily on advisor delivery and credibility
- can become fear-heavy if not delivered calmly
- product mapping may happen too quickly if prospect emotion is not explored
- does not explicitly include post-sale relationship structure in the excerpt

## 9. Relationship to Forge Core

Pedro Intelligence contributes a likely Forge Core principle:

Human need often begins as unrecognized risk.

This is broader than insurance.

If Forge models professional selling, it should model risk recognition as a
pre-product decision stage.
