# Forge Universal Sales Principles Discovery

Status: DISCOVERY

No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No UI. No Build Tree
changes. No architecture modifications.

## 1. Executive Summary

Universal sales principles are principles that remain valid even if the product,
company, technology, presentation format, market conditions or generation
changes.

The strongest universal discovery:

Professional selling is the structured development of human decision readiness.

## 2. Universal Principles

### Principle 1: Unrecognized Risk Does Not Create Action

Description:

People may have real exposure without feeling urgency.

Evidence:

- Pedro begins with risks that exist because people are alive.
- SMNYL teaches needs analysis before recommendation.
- Kinder teaches helping the prospect recognize the need.

Universal nature:

This remains true even outside financial services. People often do not act until
they understand consequence.

### Principle 2: Trust Precedes Disclosure

Description:

A prospect rarely reveals meaningful facts or feelings unless the advisor has
created credibility, safety and relevance.

Evidence:

- Pedro opens with personal credibility and recommendation-based practice.
- SMNYL uses agenda, professional approach and prospect interaction.
- Kinder makes selling yourself, company and service part of the process before
  recommendation.

Universal nature:

Any consultative process requires trust before deep information exchange.

### Principle 3: Questions Create Self-Recognition

Description:

The prospect must participate in recognizing the problem.

Evidence:

- Pedro ends by asking which existing risk the person wants administered.
- SMNYL centers the initial meeting on ANF and interactive questions.
- Kinder says need recognition is created through carefully selected questions.

Universal nature:

People are more likely to act on insight they helped produce.

### Principle 4: A Qualified Prospect Is More Than a Name

Description:

Contactability is not qualification.

Evidence:

- SMNYL uses NAST: need, assurance/insurability, solvency and decision access.
- Kinder separates contact/referred qualified from prospect qualified.
- Pedro's approach assumes the person must identify an administrable risk.

Universal nature:

Opportunity quality requires fit, access, relevance and capacity.

### Principle 5: The Sale Is a Sequence, Not a Moment

Description:

The final close is only one stage in a series of decisions.

Evidence:

- Kinder explicitly defines six sales.
- SMNYL breaks activity into prospecting, approach, ANF, commitment, closing,
  referrals and field coaching.
- Pedro moves from credibility to risk theory to risk mapping to choice.

Universal nature:

Complex decisions unfold through trust, understanding, commitment and action.

### Principle 6: Product Comes After Human Context

Description:

Product discussion is meaningful only after the prospect's situation, concerns,
capacity and priorities are understood.

Evidence:

- Pedro maps products only after discussing risk categories.
- SMNYL teaches that the first step is determining priorities and goals.
- Kinder warns against traditional product-centered selling.

Universal nature:

Solutions are persuasive when they are connected to a recognized human problem.

### Principle 7: Commitment Must Be Concrete

Description:

Interest is not the same as willingness to act.

Evidence:

- SMNYL wants most important need, monetary commitment and closing appointment.
- Kinder requires monetary commitment before preparing recommendation.
- Pedro's closing movement asks which existing risk should be administered.

Universal nature:

Decision readiness requires a move from abstract agreement to specific
commitment.

### Principle 8: Professional Process Protects Against Pressure

Description:

A structured process can reduce manipulation by making purpose, process and next
steps explicit.

Evidence:

- SMNYL teaches agenda, purpose, process and benefit.
- Kinder frames the advisor as assistant to the buyer.
- Pedro frames the conversation as determining whether he can help.

Universal nature:

Transparency increases trust in any advisory conversation.

### Principle 9: Relationship Continues After the Sale

Description:

The sale is not complete when the application or close occurs.

Evidence:

- Kinder includes solidifying the sale and developing the relationship.
- SMNYL includes referrals, field observation, activity tracking and service
  behavior.
- Pedro's recommendation-based practice depends on ongoing value.

Universal nature:

Client trust is cumulative and post-decision experience affects future
recommendations.

### Principle 10: The Advisor Is the Transfer Mechanism Between Concern and Action

Description:

The advisor helps the prospect convert latent concern into responsible action.

Evidence:

- Pedro turns risk into transfer.
- SMNYL turns prospecting into ANF, commitment and follow-up.
- Kinder turns need recognition into monetary commitment and recommendation.

Universal nature:

Professional selling is human guidance through decision friction.

## 3. Product-Specific Practices Excluded From Universal Core

The following should not be treated as universal:

- specific insurance product mappings
- SMNYL-specific compensation or training systems
- company scripts that depend on brand language
- 25-point formulas
- product certifications
- specific product illustrations
- carrier-specific underwriting language
- exact objection scripts

These may be useful rule-pack or methodology-pack content, but not Forge Core.

## 4. Durable Forge Core Candidates

Closest to universal Forge Core:

- Trust before disclosure.
- Need recognition before recommendation.
- Questions before product.
- Qualification requires need, capacity, access and willingness.
- The sale is a sequence of decisions.
- Product is the response to human context.
- Commitment must be observable.
- Relationship value outlives the transaction.
