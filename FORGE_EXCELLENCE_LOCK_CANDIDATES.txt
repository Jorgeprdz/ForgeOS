FORGE EXCELLENCE LOCK CANDIDATES

Status:
CONSTITUTIONAL LOCK CANDIDATES / NOT APPROVED

Purpose:
List candidate constitutional statements for Professional Excellence. These are
not ratified.

LOCK CANDIDATE 1

Professional Excellence is a first-class architectural concept.

Reason:
Career Stage tells Forge where a person is. Professional Excellence tells Forge
how well the person is developing within that stage.

Risk:
Requires evidence contracts and anti-surveillance boundaries.

LOCK CANDIDATE 2

Professional Excellence is not production, compensation, title, tenure, team
size, popularity or charisma.

Reason:
All can be context or evidence, but none prove excellence alone.

Risk:
Production-only culture may resist this.

LOCK CANDIDATE 3

Professional Excellence is multi-dimensional and stage-aware.

Reason:
Candidate excellence, Advisor excellence, Partner excellence and Manager
excellence require different interpretation.

Risk:
May become complex without progressive disclosure.

LOCK CANDIDATE 4

Forge evaluates observable professional signals, not human worth.

Reason:
Excellence interpretation must preserve dignity.

Risk:
Language can drift into moral judgment.

LOCK CANDIDATE 5

Professional Excellence requires evidence, confidence and context.

Reason:
Without evidence, excellence becomes opinion or narrative.

Risk:
Some dimensions such as character and trust require careful evidence handling.

LOCK CANDIDATE 6

Purpose is not motivation scoring.

Reason:
Future You Conversation and Purpose Snapshot provide human context, not
performance psychology.

Risk:
Purpose data could be misused for pressure.

LOCK CANDIDATE 7

Manager Intelligence uses excellence signals for development, not surveillance.

Reason:
Managers need coaching context, but not a tool to shame or police.

Risk:
Ranking and monitoring patterns may creep in.

LOCK CANDIDATE 8

Mick contributes behavioral evidence but does not own all Professional
Excellence.

Reason:
Mick measures actions, discipline, consistency and coachability, but excellence
also includes trust, decision quality, relationship quality, planning and
leadership.

Risk:
Behavior data may be over-weighted because it is easier to observe.

LOCK CANDIDATE 9

Professional growth is modeled as development over time, not a fixed trait.

Reason:
People can improve, plateau, regress and reactivate.

Risk:
Trend interpretation must avoid overreacting to short-term noise.

LOCK CANDIDATE 10

Excellence outputs must lead to development actions, not labels.

Reason:
Forge exists to improve decisions and action, not to classify people for its own
sake.

Risk:
Static labels can become stigma.

LOCK CANDIDATE 11

Character and trust must be handled with high evidence discipline.

Reason:
These dimensions are important but sensitive.

Risk:
Poor handling can become judgmental or unfair.

LOCK CANDIDATE 12

Professional Excellence must never become a public leaderboard by default.

Reason:
Excellence is developmental and contextual, not a simplistic competition.

Risk:
Leaderboard culture can distort behavior.

RECOMMENDED LOCK ORDER

Priority 1:
Professional Excellence is not production, compensation, title, tenure, team
size, popularity or charisma.

Priority 2:
Professional Excellence is multi-dimensional and stage-aware.

Priority 3:
Forge evaluates observable professional signals, not human worth.

Priority 4:
Manager Intelligence uses excellence signals for development, not surveillance.

Priority 5:
Purpose is not motivation scoring.

Priority 6:
Professional Excellence is a first-class architectural concept.

WHY THIS ORDER

Protective boundaries should lock before the concept label.

If Forge locks "Professional Excellence" without first protecting against
production-only evaluation, surveillance and motivational scoring, the concept
can become harmful.

FINAL VERDICT

CANDIDATE FOR LOCK.

Reason:
Professional Excellence is necessary for Career OS, but final lock should wait
for evidence contracts, dimension ownership and manager visibility guardrails.
