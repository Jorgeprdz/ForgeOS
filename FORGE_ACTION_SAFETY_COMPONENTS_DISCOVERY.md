# Forge Action Safety Components Discovery

Status: DISCOVERY

No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No UI. No Build Tree
changes. No architecture modifications. No scores. No labels.

## 1. Executive Summary

Action Safety appears to be composed of multiple safety conditions. No single
component is sufficient in every situation.

The most evidence-supported components are:

- trust
- clarity
- emotional safety
- economic clarity
- relational alignment
- decision-maker alignment
- competence confidence
- implementation confidence
- agency preservation
- acceptable uncertainty

## 2. Component 1: Trust

Definition:

The person believes the advisor, manager or counterpart is not exploiting them
and is competent enough to guide the process.

Evidence:

Kinder treats trust as central to buying. Pedro establishes credibility before
risk discussion. SMNYL uses professional approach and company credibility before
deeper needs work.

Limits:

Trust alone does not solve money, spouse alignment or confusion.

## 3. Component 2: Clarity

Definition:

The person understands the decision, the reason for action and the next step
well enough to move.

Evidence:

Kinder warns that confused prospects rarely buy and emphasizes simple,
professional presentation.

Limits:

Clarity can become endless research if not connected to ownership and action.

## 4. Component 3: Emotional Safety

Definition:

The person can face the implication of the action without feeling shamed,
manipulated, judged or emotionally overwhelmed.

Evidence:

Pedro's risk topics are emotionally heavy: illness, disability, death, living
too long and unmet goals. Hidden Frictions Discovery identified fear of regret,
looking foolish and being pressured as action blockers.

Limits:

Emotional safety should not mean avoiding hard truth.

## 5. Component 4: Economic Clarity

Definition:

The person understands whether action is financially possible and what tradeoff
is being made.

Evidence:

SMNYL and Kinder both require solvency / monetary commitment before action.

Limits:

Economic clarity may show that a different action, smaller action or no action
is responsible.

## 6. Component 5: Relational Alignment

Definition:

The relevant relationship system is not blocking action.

Examples:

- spouse
- business partner
- family member
- manager
- mentor

Evidence:

Kinder emphasizes decision-maker presence and spouse influence. Readiness
discovery identified missing decision-makers as a readiness risk.

Limits:

One person can feel action-safe individually while the household or business
does not.

## 7. Component 6: Competence Confidence

Definition:

The actor believes they can perform the action or handle the conversation.

Evidence:

SMNYL induction builds confidence through scripts, role play, 25 points and
field observation. Kinder links advisor confidence to practice, preparation and
knowledge.

Limits:

Competence confidence can become false confidence if not grounded in skill.

## 8. Component 7: Implementation Confidence

Definition:

The person understands what will happen after the decision.

Evidence:

Decision vs Transaction Discovery separated decision, application,
implementation and placement. Action can stall after decision if the
implementation path feels unsafe or unclear.

Limits:

Implementation confidence must not hide real underwriting, payment or execution
uncertainty.

## 9. Component 8: Agency Preservation

Definition:

The person feels they are choosing, not being forced.

Evidence:

Kinder criticizes pressure-based selling. SMNYL scripts include language that
reduces fear of being forced to buy.

Limits:

Preserving agency does not mean avoiding clear recommendations.

## 10. Component 9: Acceptable Uncertainty

Definition:

The person does not need certainty, but uncertainty must feel bounded,
understood and tolerable.

Evidence:

Readiness vs Friction Discovery found that high readiness can coexist with
uncertainty if the uncertainty is manageable.

Limits:

Some uncertainty is unacceptable when the consequence is too high or the
evidence is too weak.

## 11. Component Summary

Action Safety likely forms when enough of these are present:

Trust + Clarity + Emotional Safety + Economic Clarity + Relational Alignment +
Competence Confidence + Implementation Confidence + Agency Preservation +
Acceptable Uncertainty.

No score is implied.

The components are diagnostic evidence patterns, not measurements.
