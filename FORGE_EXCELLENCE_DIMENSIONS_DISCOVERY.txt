FORGE EXCELLENCE DIMENSIONS DISCOVERY

Status:
ARCHITECTURE DISCOVERY

Scope:
No implementation. No code. No UI. No schemas. No engines. No Build Tree
changes.

PURPOSE

Identify the dimensions of Professional Excellence that Forge should consider
when helping a person grow within a Career Stage or Leadership Stage.

CORE PRINCIPLE

Excellence is not one score.

Excellence is a multi-dimensional interpretation of observable, stage-aware
behaviors and decisions.

UNIVERSAL DIMENSIONS

1. Discipline

Definition:
The ability to do necessary work with reliable cadence.

Evidence examples:
- repeated activity
- commitment completion
- preparation
- operating cadence

Not evidence alone:
- intensity burst
- self-description
- one productive week

2. Consistency

Definition:
The durability of behavior over time.

Evidence examples:
- stable follow-up patterns
- repeated execution
- low avoidable drift

Risk:
Consistency must not punish healthy adaptation.

3. Follow Through

Definition:
Turning commitments, conversations and plans into completed action.

Evidence examples:
- completed next actions
- closed loops
- client follow-up
- manager commitments completed

4. Coachability

Definition:
Ability to receive, test and integrate feedback into behavior.

Evidence examples:
- behavior changes after coaching
- improved execution after feedback
- asks useful questions

Not evidence:
- agreement in conversation without action

5. Learning Ability

Definition:
Ability to improve through experience.

Evidence examples:
- fewer repeated mistakes
- better objection handling
- faster adoption of useful workflows
- improved decision quality

6. Relationship Building

Definition:
Ability to create and maintain trustworthy commercial relationships.

Evidence examples:
- engagement quality
- continuity
- referrals with context
- repeat trust signals

7. Trust

Definition:
Reliability, honesty and care in professional interactions.

Evidence examples:
- no invented claims
- transparent follow-up
- client-centered decisions
- manager/advisor reliability

8. Character

Definition:
Durable professional integrity under pressure.

Evidence examples:
- honest handling of uncertainty
- keeps boundaries
- avoids manipulation
- preserves client dignity

Boundary:
Character must be evidence-based and carefully worded.

9. Decision Quality

Definition:
Ability to choose appropriate actions based on context, evidence and tradeoffs.

Evidence examples:
- recommendation quality
- prioritization
- avoids unsupported projections
- chooses next best action with reason

10. Adaptability

Definition:
Ability to adjust behavior when context changes.

Evidence examples:
- changes approach after new evidence
- adapts messaging
- recovers from setbacks

11. Business Planning

Definition:
Ability to connect ambition, facts, forecast, behavior and action.

Evidence examples:
- realistic plans
- action paths
- plan updates after evidence
- follow-through against plan

12. Execution Quality

Definition:
The quality of completed work, not merely activity quantity.

Evidence examples:
- prepared meetings
- thoughtful follow-up
- accurate policy handling
- clean handoffs

13. Communication Quality

Definition:
Ability to communicate with clarity, empathy and commercial usefulness.

Evidence examples:
- clear next steps
- client-language explanations
- objection handling
- manager coaching conversations

14. Evidence Respect

Definition:
Willingness to operate from facts, confidence and known uncertainty.

Evidence examples:
- does not invent values
- asks for missing source
- distinguishes forecast from fact
- uses rule context

15. Client / People Stewardship

Definition:
Quality of responsibility toward clients, advisors, candidates or teams.

Evidence examples:
- protects client interest
- supports team development
- respects candidate dignity
- follows through on people commitments

DIMENSIONS TO AVOID OR RENAME

"Motivation":
Avoid as a dimension. Use observable drive, activity, commitment or purpose
context instead.

"Popularity":
Reject. It is not excellence.

"Charisma":
Reject as a dimension. It may help communication but can hide weak trust.

"Production":
Use as evidence or outcome, not an excellence dimension.

"Leadership":
Split into leadership responsibility and leadership quality.
