# Forge Action Safety vs Friction Discovery

Status: DISCOVERY

No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No UI. No Build Tree
changes. No architecture modifications. No scores. No labels.

## 1. Executive Summary

Friction and Action Safety are tightly related, but not identical.

Friction asks:

"What force is slowing or blocking action?"

Action Safety asks:

"What condition would make action feel responsible enough to take?"

## 2. Does Friction Destroy Action Safety?

Often, yes.

Examples:

- confusion destroys clarity
- fear of regret destroys decision confidence
- spouse absence destroys relational alignment
- price ambiguity destroys economic safety
- pressure destroys agency
- rejection fear destroys advisor action safety
- conflict fear destroys manager action safety

But not all friction destroys Action Safety. Some friction is normal effort.

Example:

An advisor may dislike calls but still feel safe enough to make them because
they are prepared and coached.

## 3. Does Action Safety Reduce Friction?

Often, yes.

Examples:

- clear next step reduces procrastination
- monetary clarity reduces price anxiety
- decision-maker alignment reduces relational delay
- prepared language reduces advisor fear
- coaching structure reduces manager avoidance
- implementation clarity reduces document delay

But Action Safety does not remove real constraints.

Example:

If the prospect truly lacks economic capacity, safety may clarify that action
should change or pause.

## 4. Friction Without Lack of Safety

Some friction is not safety-related:

- logistics
- scheduling
- legitimate budget limit
- underwriting wait
- unavailable documents
- timing conflict

These may still block action, but the person may feel safe and simply need path
resolution.

## 5. Lack of Safety Without Visible Friction

Action Safety can be low before friction becomes visible.

Examples:

- prospect smiles but does not disclose
- advisor says they will call but avoids
- manager says they will coach but delays
- client agrees but goes silent

Silent safety gaps can become visible later as delay.

## 6. Final Finding

Friction explains the drag.

Action Safety explains what would make movement feel responsible.

They should remain separate discovery concepts.
