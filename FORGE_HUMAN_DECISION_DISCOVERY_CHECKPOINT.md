# Forge Human Decision Discovery Checkpoint

Status: CHECKPOINT REVIEW

Documentation only. No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No UI.
No Build Tree changes. No architecture modifications.

## 1. Executive Summary

This checkpoint preserves the learning from:

- Professional Sales Process Discovery
- Universal Sales Principles Discovery
- Readiness Intelligence Discovery
- Friction Intelligence Discovery
- Action Safety Discovery

The consolidated finding is:

Forge should understand professional selling and related human development work
as a decision-readiness process, not a product-presentation process.

Humans move toward action through a sequence:

Awareness -> Recognition -> Ownership -> Commitment -> Decision -> Action

But that sequence is not automatic. Readiness can exist without action. Friction
can block action. Action Safety appears to explain why a person may know and
want to act, but still not move.

## 2. What Forge Believed Before

Before these discoveries, Forge's working assumptions were closer to:

- product presentation matters heavily
- objections explain much of stalled action
- readiness is primarily a pre-action condition
- sales process is mostly advisor workflow
- action follows need recognition if the recommendation is strong

These were not wrong, but incomplete.

## 3. What Forge Believes Now

After discovery, Forge has stronger evidence for:

- product follows human context
- professional selling develops decision readiness
- the prospect timeline differs from the advisor timeline
- the transaction may occur after the psychological decision begins
- readiness is evidence-pattern based, not a score
- friction is not simply lack of motivation
- action safety may be the missing condition between decision and action
- ethical boundaries are required before any intelligence interprets human
  decision signals

## 4. Strongly Supported Findings

### Product Follows Human Context

Supported by Pedro, SMNYL and Kinder.

Products become meaningful after risk, need, trust, capacity and context are
understood.

### Trust Before Disclosure

Supported by all reviewed sources.

Meaningful discovery requires enough trust for the prospect to share facts,
feelings and constraints.

### Need Recognition Before Recommendation

Supported by all reviewed sources.

Recommendation has weak force if the prospect has not recognized relevance or
need.

### The Sale Is a Sequence of Decisions

Strongly supported by Kinder and reinforced by SMNYL process structure.

The final close is not the only sale.

### Readiness Is Evidence Pattern, Not Score

Supported by Forge Readiness discoveries.

Readiness requires contextual evidence and must not become a label.

### Friction Can Exist With Readiness

Supported by Readiness and Friction discoveries.

People can be ready in principle and still blocked from action.

### Action Safety Is a Plausible Decision Concept

Supported by Friction and Action Safety discoveries.

People often need sufficient safety, not perfect certainty, to act responsibly.

## 5. Findings That Remain Hypotheses

- Action Safety as a universal human decision concept.
- The exact sequence Awareness -> Recognition -> Ownership -> Commitment ->
  Decision -> Action.
- Whether Action Safety should become Forge Core, Sales Intelligence or Career
  OS language.
- Whether friction patterns are universal across clients, advisors, managers,
  recruits and partners.
- Whether first-meeting readiness can be reliably observed without transcripts.
- Whether monetary commitment is universal or financial-services-specific.

## 6. Findings Requiring Real-World Validation

Require real-world validation:

- readiness signals in actual client meetings
- false readiness vs real readiness
- friction signals across follow-up behavior
- Action Safety components in live conversations
- whether implementation questions predict action
- whether hidden friction can be identified ethically
- whether advisor confidence overstates prospect readiness
- whether manager coaching friction follows the same pattern as client friction

## 7. Findings That May Influence Future Forge Architecture

Potential future influence:

- Sales Intelligence may need to model client decision state before product
  recommendation.
- Relationship Intelligence may need to distinguish follow-up friction from low
  interest.
- Advisor Development Intelligence may need to detect advisor-side friction.
- Manager Intelligence may need to distinguish coaching avoidance from capacity
  gaps.
- Product Intelligence may need to remain downstream of human context.
- Article 0 and Skynet reviews may need to protect agency and prevent
  compliance optimization.

This checkpoint does not approve any architecture modification.

## 8. Findings That Should Remain Methodology-Level Only

Methodology-level only:

- exact SMNYL scripts
- exact Kinder six-sales terminology
- exact Pedro risk script phrasing
- NAST as company-specific qualification language
- SMNYL 25-point activity format
- exact objection responses
- specific first-meeting and second-meeting script order

These may inform methodology packs but should not be hardcoded as Forge Core.

## 9. Closest to Forge Core

Closest to Forge Core:

- Product follows human context.
- Need recognition precedes responsible recommendation.
- Decision clarity matters before action.
- Friction can block action despite readiness.
- Action requires sufficient safety, not perfect certainty.
- Human agency must be preserved.
- Forge should improve judgment and decision quality, not produce compliance.
