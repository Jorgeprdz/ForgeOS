# Forge Action Safety Destroyers Discovery

Status: DISCOVERY

No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No UI. No Build Tree
changes. No architecture modifications. No scores. No labels.

## 1. Executive Summary

Action Safety is reduced when acting feels likely to create unacceptable harm,
regret, pressure, embarrassment, conflict, loss of options or confusion.

The strongest destroyers found across the sources and prior discoveries are:

- confusion
- overwhelm
- fear of regret
- fear of embarrassment
- fear of rejection
- fear of pressure
- fear of losing options
- fear of conflict
- economic ambiguity
- missing decision-makers
- weak next step
- low implementation confidence

## 2. Destroyer 1: Confusion

Description:

The person does not understand enough to act.

Evidence:

Kinder emphasizes that confused prospects rarely buy and that presentation
power comes from simplicity.

Effect:

Action feels unsafe because the person cannot predict what they are agreeing
to.

## 3. Destroyer 2: Overwhelm

Description:

The decision feels too large, complex or emotionally heavy.

Evidence:

Friction Discovery identified cognitive overload and emotional avoidance.

Effect:

Action is delayed because the person cannot process the decision safely.

## 4. Destroyer 3: Fear of Regret

Description:

The person fears later discovering they chose wrong.

Evidence:

Hidden Frictions Discovery identified fear of regret as a likely hidden blocker
behind "let me think about it" and endless comparison.

Effect:

Action stalls even after agreement.

## 5. Destroyer 4: Fear of Embarrassment

Description:

The person fears looking foolish, irresponsible or financially unprepared.

Evidence:

Hidden Frictions Discovery identified fear of looking irresponsible and fear of
being judged.

Effect:

Disclosure and commitment become unsafe.

## 6. Destroyer 5: Fear of Rejection

Description:

Advisor or manager avoids action because the response could be negative.

Evidence:

Kinder describes telephone resistance as often caused by fear of rejection.
Advisor Friction Discovery found follow-up and prospecting avoidance.

Effect:

The advisor knows what to do but does not act.

## 7. Destroyer 6: Fear of Pressure

Description:

The person feels action may be coerced.

Evidence:

Kinder contrasts professional selling with pressure that creates defensive
behavior. SMNYL scripts reassure prospects that the meeting is not to force a
purchase.

Effect:

The person protects agency by delaying or resisting.

## 8. Destroyer 7: Fear of Losing Options

Description:

Commitment feels like closing off future flexibility.

Evidence:

Friction Pattern Library identified option preservation as a friction pattern.

Effect:

The person delays action to preserve possibility.

## 9. Destroyer 8: Fear of Conflict

Description:

Action may create disagreement with spouse, partner, client, advisor or team
member.

Evidence:

Manager Friction Discovery identified difficult conversation avoidance and
conflict avoidance.

Effect:

Leadership or client action is delayed.

## 10. Destroyer 9: Economic Ambiguity

Description:

The person does not understand affordability, tradeoff or budget impact.

Evidence:

SMNYL and Kinder both require monetary commitment / solvency before
recommendation and action.

Effect:

Action feels financially unsafe.

## 11. Destroyer 10: Missing Decision-Maker

Description:

The person cannot safely act alone.

Evidence:

Kinder emphasizes decision-maker context and spouse influence. Readiness
Discovery identified missing decision-makers as a readiness gap.

Effect:

Individual desire fails to become system-level action.

## 12. Destroyer 11: Weak Next Step

Description:

The decision lacks a concrete action path.

Evidence:

SMNYL and Kinder emphasize scheduled next appointments, monetary commitment and
close structure.

Effect:

Agreement evaporates into delay.

## 13. Destroyer 12: Low Implementation Confidence

Description:

The person does not know what happens after yes.

Evidence:

Decision vs Transaction Discovery separated decision from application and
implementation.

Effect:

Action stalls after decision.
