# Forge Action Safety vs Readiness Discovery

Status: DISCOVERY

No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No UI. No Build Tree
changes. No architecture modifications. No scores. No labels.

## 1. Executive Summary

Readiness and Action Safety are related but distinct.

Readiness asks:

"Is this person prepared to move toward this responsibility or action?"

Action Safety asks:

"Does moving now feel sufficiently safe to do responsibly?"

## 2. Does Action Safety Create Readiness?

Sometimes.

Example:

A prospect has awareness but not commitment because the conversation feels
pressured. When agency and trust improve, readiness may form.

But Action Safety does not automatically create readiness. A person can feel
safe in the conversation and still not recognize the need.

## 3. Does Readiness Create Action Safety?

Sometimes.

Example:

A prospect owns the need and has monetary commitment. That can make action feel
safer because the decision is coherent.

But readiness does not automatically create Action Safety. A person can be ready
in principle but blocked by fear, spouse misalignment, economic ambiguity or
implementation uncertainty.

## 4. Can Readiness Exist Without Action Safety?

Yes.

Examples:

- "I know I need this, but I am afraid of making the wrong decision."
- "I want to proceed, but I need my spouse involved."
- "This matters, but I do not understand what happens next."
- "I should follow up, but I fear sounding pushy."
- "I need to coach this advisor, but I fear conflict."

## 5. Can Action Safety Exist Without Readiness?

Yes.

Examples:

- comfortable conversation with no need ownership
- trusted advisor but no urgency
- clear process but no economic capacity
- safe coaching environment but no responsibility readiness

Safety alone does not create the need to act.

## 6. Relationship Model

Readiness:

Need + ownership + capacity + commitment evidence.

Action Safety:

Trust + clarity + acceptable uncertainty + agency + implementation confidence.

Action:

Readiness + sufficient Action Safety + available path.

## 7. Final Finding

Readiness can point toward action.

Action Safety determines whether the person can cross the action threshold
without feeling exposed, trapped, pressured or overwhelmed.
