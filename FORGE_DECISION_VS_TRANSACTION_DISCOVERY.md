# Forge Decision vs Transaction Discovery

Status: DISCOVERY

No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No UI. No Build Tree
changes. No architecture modifications. No readiness scores. No readiness
labels.

## 1. Executive Summary

Decision, commitment, closing, application, implementation and policy placement
are not identical events.

They may happen close together, but they represent different human and
operational moments.

The mistake is to treat the signed application as the moment the client first
decided. In many professional sales processes, the client has already crossed
several internal decision thresholds before any document is signed.

## 2. Definitions

### Decision

The internal human movement from:

"This may be relevant"

to:

"I should do something about this."

Decision is psychological and contextual. It may be invisible unless expressed
through language, questions, commitment or action.

### Commitment

An observable expression that the prospect is willing to allocate something:

- money
- time
- attention
- information
- next meeting
- decision-maker involvement
- implementation effort

Commitment is evidence that decision may exist, but it is not transaction.

### Closing

The advisor-led moment where the recommendation is connected to action and the
prospect is asked to proceed.

Closing tests whether readiness becomes action.

### Application

The administrative act of submitting formal information for underwriting,
approval or issuance.

Application is evidence of action, but not always proof of stable decision.

### Implementation

The practical execution of the chosen solution:

- forms
- underwriting
- payment
- delivery
- setup
- service handoff

Implementation can reveal unresolved doubts.

### Policy Placement

The operational completion of a policy becoming active, delivered, accepted and
paid according to applicable process.

Policy placement is transaction completion, not necessarily the origin of the
decision.

## 3. Are They Identical?

No.

They can align in simple cases, but professional sales discovery indicates they
are separate:

- decision can occur before commitment
- commitment can occur before recommendation
- closing can happen after decision
- application can happen after close
- implementation can fail despite apparent decision
- policy placement can occur after multiple readiness confirmations

## 4. Evidence From Sources

Pedro:

Decision begins when the prospect accepts risk exists and chooses to administer
it.

SMNYL:

First meeting captures detected needs agreement and monetary commitment. Second
meeting presents solution and closes.

Kinder:

Need recognition and monetary commitment precede recommendation and closing.
The process explicitly separates the buyer's decision path from the
presentation.

## 5. Decision vs Transaction Risks

Risk 1:

Advisor mistakes transaction for decision.

Effect:

The advisor focuses only on close mechanics and misses need ownership.

Risk 2:

Advisor mistakes interest for decision.

Effect:

The advisor enters the second meeting assuming readiness that does not exist.

Risk 3:

Advisor mistakes application for stable commitment.

Effect:

Post-application doubts, cancellations or weak persistence may appear.

## 6. Best Evidence-Supported Distinction

Decision:

"I should act."

Commitment:

"I am willing to allocate resources to act."

Closing:

"Will you act on this recommendation now?"

Application:

"We are submitting the required formal request."

Implementation:

"We are making the chosen solution operational."

Policy placement:

"The transaction is completed and in force."
