# Forge Professional Sales Process Discovery

Status: DISCOVERY

No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No UI. No Build Tree
changes. No architecture modifications.

## 1. Executive Summary

This discovery compares three sources:

- Pedro Camarena Intelligence: Guion Riesgos
- Corporate Methodology: Manual de Induccion
- Kinder Methodology: Vendiendo Exitosamente

The central finding:

Professional selling is not primarily a product delivery process. It is a human
decision-development process.

Across the sources, the durable problem being solved is not "how to sell
insurance." The durable problem is:

Most people live exposed to important financial risks without fully recognizing,
prioritizing or acting on them.

The advisor's role is to help the prospect move from unexamined exposure to
recognized need, trusted conversation, concrete commitment and responsible
action.

## 2. Source Positions

### Pedro Camarena Intelligence

Pedro's risk script frames the conversation around risk transfer.

The core movement:

1. Establish personal credibility.
2. Explain that the advisor works by recommendation.
3. Introduce the theory of risks.
4. Distinguish mild, moderate and catastrophic risks.
5. Explain that catastrophic means economically damaging.
6. Show that some risks cannot be eliminated or fully reduced.
7. Present transfer as the responsible strategy.
8. Map human risks to possible financial tools.
9. Ask which existing risk the person wants administered.

Underlying philosophy:

People are exposed to risks simply by being alive. The sale begins when the
prospect recognizes that risk is already present before a product is discussed.

### Corporate Methodology

The induction manual frames professional selling as a managed advisor operating
system.

The core movement:

1. Build disciplined activity.
2. Create a qualified prospect base.
3. Use scripts for approach and objections.
4. Schedule appointments.
5. Conduct needs analysis.
6. Identify the most important need.
7. Secure monetary commitment.
8. Schedule closing.
9. Generate qualified referrals.
10. Learn through observation and field demonstration.

Underlying philosophy:

Professional selling is repeatable behavior supported by activity discipline,
prospecting systems, field coaching and structured conversations.

### Kinder Methodology

Kinder frames the process as a sequence of sales inside the client's buying
process.

The core movement:

1. Get an appointment under favorable conditions.
2. Sell yourself, the company, need recognition and service.
3. Obtain monetary commitment.
4. Present simply and professionally, then close.
5. Solidify the sale and develop the relationship.
6. Make the sale productive through referrals.

Underlying philosophy:

The prospect is not a professional buyer of financial services. The advisor must
assist the prospect through a series of decisions that make responsible action
possible.

## 3. What Problem Does the Sales Process Solve?

The sales process solves a human decision problem:

People do not automatically convert future risk into present action.

The process helps the prospect:

- notice risk
- understand consequence
- connect risk to personal goals
- trust the advisor enough to disclose information
- decide whether the problem matters now
- identify whether action is financially possible
- choose a recommended course
- commit before the future problem becomes a present crisis

## 4. What Occurs Before Product Discussion?

Across the sources, several things must happen before product discussion becomes
productive:

- personal credibility
- trust formation
- reason for conversation
- risk or need recognition
- prospect participation
- disclosure of facts
- disclosure of feelings
- prioritization of need
- evidence of economic capacity
- willingness to continue
- advisor belief and preparation

Pedro begins with personal credibility and risk recognition.

SMNYL begins operationally with prospect qualification, approach, agenda and
needs analysis.

Kinder begins with the appointment, positioning, trust, need recognition and
service value.

## 5. Repeated Principles

Repeated principles across the sources:

- The prospect must recognize a need or risk before the recommendation matters.
- Trust must precede meaningful disclosure.
- The advisor's personal credibility matters.
- The sale is a process, not an isolated presentation.
- Product discussion should follow human context.
- The advisor must ask questions, not only explain products.
- Referral quality matters more than raw names.
- A qualified prospect is not merely a reachable person.
- Economic capacity and willingness to act matter.
- The advisor must help the prospect move from awareness to action.
- Follow-through and activity discipline are essential.

## 6. Repeated Advisor Behaviors

Repeated advisor behaviors:

- prepare before contact
- approach with purpose
- establish credibility
- respect the prospect's time
- ask questions
- listen for facts and feelings
- help the prospect verbalize need
- identify the decision-maker
- clarify economic capacity
- set next steps
- handle objections without attacking
- ask for referrals professionally
- use field practice and coaching

## 7. Repeated Prospect Behaviors

Repeated prospect behaviors:

- begins with low awareness or vague awareness
- may resist because of time, money, prior advisor or low interest
- becomes more open when respected and understood
- discloses more when trust exists
- starts to care when personal risk or desire becomes clear
- moves toward action when benefit, trust and capacity align
- often needs help making the decision sequence explicit

## 8. Product-Specific vs Universal

Product-specific practices:

- mapping illness risk to GMM
- mapping disability risk to disability insurance
- mapping living too long to retirement or savings solutions
- product certifications
- cotizadores
- company-specific scripts
- SMNYL activity point formulas
- product-specific quote presentation

Universal principles:

- people avoid uncomfortable future-risk thinking
- trust unlocks disclosure
- questions create self-recognition
- action requires personal relevance
- commitment precedes recommendation quality
- relationship can be a competitive advantage
- professional process reduces pressure
- service continues after purchase

## 9. When Does the Sale Really Occur?

The sources do not fully agree if "sale" means final policy placement.

But they converge that the decisive sale begins before the formal close.

Pedro:

The sale begins when the person accepts that risks already exist and chooses
which risk they want administered.

SMNYL:

The sale begins operationally when a qualified appointment turns into needs
analysis, most important need, monetary commitment and closing appointment.

Kinder:

The sale occurs as a sequence of six sales. The client is progressively sold on
the appointment, advisor, company, need, service, monetary commitment,
recommendation, relationship and referrals.

Discovery conclusion:

The sale "really" occurs in stages. The critical human sale occurs at need
recognition plus trust plus willingness to commit.

## 10. True Competitive Advantage

The sources point to a consistent competitive advantage:

The advisor's ability to create trust, help the prospect understand themselves,
and guide action responsibly.

Product, price and company matter, but they are not sufficient.

Pedro emphasizes field credibility and risk framing.

SMNYL emphasizes company credibility, process, activity and professional
discipline.

Kinder explicitly emphasizes relationship, trust and the advisor's role as
assistant to the buyer.

## 11. Client-Centered Selling

Client-centered selling is observable when the advisor:

- starts from the prospect's situation
- asks before recommending
- helps the prospect identify their own priorities
- avoids forcing a product into every conversation
- explains process and benefit before collecting data
- connects recommendations to stated goals and risks
- seeks monetary commitment only after need recognition
- handles objections as information, not combat
- preserves the prospect's sense of agency
- develops the relationship after the transaction

Client-centered selling is not just friendly tone. It is a process in which the
prospect's facts, feelings, priorities and decision readiness shape the path.

## 12. Hidden Assumptions

Pedro hidden assumptions:

- People already have risks even if they have not named them.
- Risk transfer is healthier than pretending risk can be eliminated.
- The advisor earns relevance by helping clients avoid painful lessons.
- Recommendation-based practice signals trust and value.

SMNYL hidden assumptions:

- Professional activity can be systematized.
- New advisors need scripts, repetition, observation and measurable activity.
- Prospecting quality determines future sales flow.
- Manager coaching is necessary for skill formation.

Kinder hidden assumptions:

- Prospects need assistance to buy because they are not professional buyers.
- Need recognition is not automatic.
- Trust and liking strongly influence action.
- Selling is a sequence of decisions, not one pitch.
- The advisor must be persuasive, but not product-centered.

## 13. Final Answer

If all financial products disappeared tomorrow, the following parts would still
function:

- trust formation
- personal credibility
- identifying human goals and risks
- asking questions that create self-recognition
- distinguishing names from qualified prospects
- helping people prioritize
- moving from vague concern to concrete commitment
- handling resistance with respect
- creating next steps
- developing relationships after the decision
- earning referrals through delivered value

Those principles are closest to Forge Core.
