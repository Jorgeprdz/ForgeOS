# Forge Prospect Decision Timeline Discovery

Status: DISCOVERY

No implementation. No code. No engines. No schemas. No UI. No Build Tree
changes. No architecture modifications. No readiness scores. No readiness
labels.

## 1. Executive Summary

The advisor timeline and prospect timeline are different.

The advisor sees meetings, scripts, ANF, presentation, closing and application.

The prospect experiences attention, trust, relevance, ownership, commitment,
clarity and action.

This distinction supports the hypothesis that the transaction may occur in the
second meeting while the decision begins or largely forms in the first.

## 2. Advisor Timeline

Advisor timeline:

1. identify qualified name
2. approach
3. schedule appointment
4. conduct first meeting
5. establish agenda
6. gather facts and feelings
7. identify needs
8. secure monetary commitment
9. schedule closing appointment
10. prepare recommendation
11. present solution
12. handle objections
13. close
14. submit application
15. implement / place policy
16. service and referrals

## 3. Prospect Timeline

Prospect timeline:

1. decide whether to listen
2. decide whether the advisor is safe
3. decide whether the topic is relevant
4. recognize a need or risk
5. feel personal consequence
6. disclose enough truth
7. decide whether action is possible
8. decide whether the advisor can help
9. commit time, money or attention
10. evaluate recommendation fit
11. act or defer
12. seek post-decision confidence

## 4. Timeline Mismatch

The advisor may think:

"The sale happens when I present and close."

The prospect may experience:

"I decided this mattered when I recognized the risk and accepted I needed to do
something."

This mismatch explains why skilled advisors can sense the sale before the
formal close.

## 5. Evidence From Pedro

Pedro's timeline:

Credibility -> risk theory -> catastrophic risk recognition -> transfer logic ->
which risk should be administered.

Prospect decision movement:

"I have a risk" -> "it can hurt me economically" -> "it should be transferred or
administered."

Product presentation is not required for the first movement. It is required to
turn the movement into a specific solution.

## 6. Evidence From SMNYL

SMNYL first meeting:

- purpose, process, benefit
- priorities
- ANF
- agreement of detected needs
- monetary commitment
- referrals
- next appointment

SMNYL second meeting:

- solution presentation
- benefits
- objections
- review detected needs
- confirm monetary commitment
- close

This indicates that first meeting readiness is expected before second meeting
transaction.

## 7. Evidence From Kinder

Kinder separates:

- appointment under favorable conditions
- trust / need / service
- monetary commitment
- recommendation preparation
- presentation and close

Kinder's sequence makes decision formation prior to formal recommendation a
normal part of the process.

## 8. Most Evidence-Supported Sequence

Awareness -> Recognition -> Ownership -> Trustful Disclosure -> Commitment ->
Recommendation Fit -> Action -> Implementation -> Post-Decision Confidence

This sequence is not a score and not a fixed engine model.

It is a discovery candidate for understanding human readiness.
